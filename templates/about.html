Skin Spots & moles

A mole or nevus is a dark spot on the skin formed by groups of melanocytes, cells producing melanin. They result from sun exposure or are present at birth. Fair-skinned individuals tend to have more moles due to lower melanin levels. Adults usually have 10 to 40 moles on average, which can change with age, hormonal shifts, and sun exposure. While most moles are harmless, regular skin checks (monthly, or every three months, especially with a family history of skin cancer) are crucial to monitor any changes.

Cancerous mole?

Warning signs that it may be cancerous:
# Changes in size(getting larger)
# Itchiness, pain or bleeding
# Colour changes(getting darker or multiple shades)

ABCDE checklist

The ABCDE checklist is a simple tool for identifying potential signs of skin cancer:

  • A - Asymmetry: Check if one half of the mole or spot does not match the other half.
  • B - Border irregularity: Look for uneven or irregular edges.
  • C - Color: Pay attention to variations in color, such as different shades of brown, black, or even red.
  • D - Diameter: Check for growth or any mole larger than 6 millimeters (about the size of a pencil eraser).
  • E - Evolving: for changes over time, including size, shape, or color.
